## Foundational Strategy Frameworks

### Product-Market Fit (PMF)
- **Sean Ellis Test**: "How would you feel if you could no longer use [product]?" — target >40% "Very Disappointed"
- **Superhuman methodology**: segment users by "Very Disappointed" cohort; identify what they love AND what their profile looks like; build for the core; ignore the rest
- **Retention curve**: PMF signal = retention curve flattens (doesn't go to zero); benchmark by segment
- **Leading indicators**: DAU/MAU ratio, time-to-value, repeat usage within 1 week, "aha moment" completion rate

### Jobs-to-Be-Done (JTBD)
- People "hire" products to make progress in their lives; understand the functional, emotional, and social job
- **Forces**: progress-making forces (push of the situation + pull of new product) vs. staying forces (habit + anxiety)
- JTBD interview: "Walk me through the last time you..." — find switching context, not attribute preferences
- Apply: design onboarding around the aha moment; build features around the core job, not edge cases

### Platform vs. Pipeline vs. Network Effect
- **Pipeline**: linear value chain (producer → company → consumer); traditional product
- **Platform**: multi-sided; facilitates interactions between producers and consumers; Shopify, Stripe
- **Network effects taxonomy (NFx)**: direct (Slack), indirect (Apple App Store), data (Google Maps), social (Instagram), marketplace (Airbnb), language (English), belief (crypto)
- Build for: which network effect type can you realistically build? What's the chicken-and-egg solution?

### Flywheel Construction
- Define the central flywheel: what action creates value that attracts more users that creates more value?
- Amazon flywheel: lower prices → more customers → more sellers → lower costs → lower prices
- AI flywheel: more users → more data → better model → better product → more users
- Document your flywheel explicitly; make it the north star for all product decisions

## Go-to-Market Strategy

### ICP Definition (Ideal Customer Profile)
Define with precision: company size, industry, tech stack, team structure, budget, pain intensity, decision-maker title, trigger events (hiring, fundraise, compliance deadline, tech migration).

ICP evolution: start narrow (10 identical customers) → prove model → expand to adjacent ICPs.

### Sales Motion Selection
| Motion | Best When | Metrics |
|--------|-----------|---------|
| **PLG** (Product-Led Growth) | Low ACV (<$10K), self-serve usable product, viral potential | PQL rate, activation rate, expansion MRR |
| **SLG** (Sales-Led Growth) | Complex product, high ACV (>$50K), regulated industries | Win rate, sales cycle, AE productivity |
| **MLG** (Marketing-Led Growth) | Strong brand, content moat, high awareness | MQL→SQL conversion, pipeline coverage |
| **CLG** (Community-Led Growth) | Developer tools, bottoms-up adoption, ecosystem play | Community engagement, NPS, expansion |
| **Hybrid** | Mid-market ($10-50K ACV), self-serve + sales assist | Free→paid conversion, sales assist rate |

### Pricing Strategy
- **Value-based pricing**: anchor to value delivered, not cost; know your economic buyer's ROI equation
- **Pricing axes**: per seat, per usage, per outcome, per workspace/org, feature-tiered
- **Good-better-best**: 3 tiers; middle tier should be the obvious choice; use decoy pricing
- **Land and expand**: low entry price → easy adoption → expand with usage/seats/features
- **PLG pricing formula**: Free (100% features subset) → Pro ($20-50/user/mo) → Team ($50-100) → Enterprise (custom)
- AI product pricing: usage-based (per task/token/query) increasingly common; aligns with value; challenges: revenue predictability

### Competitive Positioning
- **Positioning statement formula**: For [target customer] who [has problem], [product name] is a [category] that [unique benefit]. Unlike [alternative], we [key differentiator].
- **Positioning map**: plot competitors on 2 key axes relevant to your market; find white space
- **Jobs-based differentiation**: compete on different jobs (new market), or better at same job (niche market), or cheaper at same job (commoditization — avoid)
- **Category creation**: name the new category you're defining; educate the market on why existing solutions fail; become the default reference point

## AI-Native Startup Strategy (2024-2025)

### The "Wrapper" Problem
The market has become skeptical of LLM "wrappers" with no differentiation. Build defensibility through:
1. **Proprietary data moat** — data others can't access; training/fine-tuning advantage
2. **Workflow integration** — deeply embedded in existing workflows; high switching cost
3. **Network effects** — your product gets better as more customers use it
4. **Vertical expertise** — deep domain knowledge that makes generic AI worse (Harvey Law, Rad AI)
5. **Distribution moat** — own a channel (platform partnership, community, brand)
6. **Speed** — first-mover + continuous improvement; move faster than foundation model companies

### Vertical AI Strategy
- Pick a vertical with: high willingness to pay, specialized data, regulatory/compliance complexity, expert knowledge required
- Best verticals: legal, healthcare, finance, construction, manufacturing, government
- Pattern: replace expensive expert labor with AI; price at 20-30% of human cost; deliver 80% of quality
- Moat: proprietary vertical training data + domain-specific evals + customer workflow lock-in

### AI-Powered Distribution
- Use AI to personalize outreach at scale (not spam — relevant, researched)
- AI content engine: generate SEO articles, social content, case studies at high volume
- Build an AI-powered freemium product that creates viral loops (Gamma, Canva AI, Notion AI)

## Brand Strategy

### Brand Architecture
- **Branded house**: single brand across all products (Apple, Google) — clear, scalable
- **House of brands**: separate brands per product (P&G) — flexibility, no cross-contamination
- **Endorsed brands**: parent brand + sub-brand (Marriott Courtyard) — hybrid
- For startups: branded house; build one strong brand before expanding

### Positioning & Messaging Hierarchy
1. **One-liner** (10 words): what you do and for whom
2. **Tagline** (5 words): memorable, emotional, distinctive
3. **Elevator pitch** (30 seconds): problem → solution → unique insight → traction
4. **Messaging pillars** (3 claims): what you want customers to believe
5. **Proof points**: evidence for each pillar (metrics, testimonials, case studies)
6. **Objection handling**: top 3 objections; direct answers

### Brand Identity System
- **Logo**: wordmark + icon; works at 16px and 16ft; test in black/white first
- **Color**: primary (recognition), secondary (support), semantic (error/success/warning)
- **Typography**: 1-2 typefaces; hierarchy: display → heading → body → caption
- **Motion**: brand timing curves; transitions communicate brand personality
- **Voice/tone**: 3-5 adjectives; do/don't lists; example phrases
- Tools: Figma (design), Linear-style minimalism is currently the dominant AI startup aesthetic

### Tech Brand Trends (2024-2025)
- Minimalist, technical, "tool-forward" (Linear, Vercel, Railway style)
- Anti-hype: understated confidence; show, don't tell
- Dark mode default with considered light mode
- Developer-first brands: code snippets as marketing, GitHub star counts on landing pages
- "Calm design" movement: less animations, faster, focus on content

## Growth Strategy

### Growth Loops vs. Funnels
- **Funnels** = linear; paid acquisition → convert → retain; expensive; breaks with ad fatigue
- **Loops** = compounding; each output becomes next cycle's input; viral, content, data, product loops
- Types: **Viral loop** (invite → new user → invite more), **Content loop** (create → SEO → traffic → more creators), **Data loop** (usage → better product → more usage), **Sales loop** (customer → expansion → referral → new customer)
- Build your primary loop first; secondary loops amplify

### SEO Strategy for SaaS
- **Programmatic SEO**: generate thousands of pages for long-tail keywords (Zapier's integrations pages model)
- **Product-led content**: tools/calculators/templates that rank AND drive product signups
- **Comparison pages**: [Your Product] vs. [Competitor]; captures high-intent searchers
- **Job-to-be-done content**: "how to do X" where X is exactly what your product does

### Community-Led Growth
- Build around the job/problem/identity, not the product; Discord, Slack, Circle
- Give community members status: titles, early access, speaker slots, swag
- Community → product feedback → better product → stronger community (flywheel)
- Examples: Linear (design/engineering community), Vercel (Next.js ecosystem), Hugging Face (ML community)

## Fundraising Narrative

### Deck Structure (Sequoia-inspired)
1. **Company purpose** — one-liner; what you do
2. **Problem** — status quo is broken; quantify the pain; make it feel urgent
3. **Solution** — your insight + approach; why now; why different
4. **Why now** — tailwinds; market timing; enabling technology
5. **Market size** — TAM/SAM/SOM; bottom-up preferred; show the path from today to massive
6. **Product** — demo or screenshots; show, don't tell
7. **Traction** — metrics that show PMF; growth trajectory; key customers
8. **Team** — unfair advantages; why you specifically can win this
9. **Business model** — how you make money; unit economics
10. **Ask** — amount, use of funds, milestones this round achieves

### Investor Narrative Principles
- Lead with the insight/secret (Thiel: what do you know that others don't?)
- Make the market feel inevitable AND large; help investors see the vision you see
- Let traction do the heavy lifting; metrics > claims
- Acknowledge the risks; investors know them; showing you understand builds trust
- The best decks tell a story; each slide answers a question the previous one raised

## Creative Execution

### Launch Strategy (2024 Playbook)
1. **Pre-launch**: build waitlist via Twitter/X content, Reddit seeding, newsletter; insider access creates exclusivity
2. **Product Hunt launch**: coordinate upvotes day-of; post on Tuesday/Wednesday; hunter with audience matters; respond to every comment
3. **HN Show HN**: technical depth; be honest about limitations; engage actively first hour
4. **Newsletter**: industry newsletters (The Pragmatic Engineer, TLDR Tech, etc.); sponsor or earn coverage
5. **Demo video**: <90 seconds; show the "wow" moment; Loom or professional depending on budget

### Content Strategy Framework
- **Pillars**: 3-5 content themes that map to your positioning pillars
- **Distribution**: own channel (blog, newsletter) + earned (PR, guest posts) + social (Twitter/X, LinkedIn)
- **Cadence**: consistency >volume; weekly newsletter, 3-5 social posts/week
- **Format mix**: long-form (authority) + short-form (reach) + video (engagement)
